# Vendor Trust & Safety Model

## Goal
Help buyers avoid wasting time or taking unnecessary risk with weakly known vendors.

## What safety review should do
- surface public warning signs
- surface identity inconsistencies
- show business-footprint evidence
- recommend manual caution steps

## What safety review should not do
- make definitive accusations
- silently suppress a lead based on weak evidence
- replace human judgment

## Example safety flags
- new_domain
- no_business_footprint
- conflicting_contact_info
- complaint_reports_found
- suspicious_web_presence
- unverifiable_address
- unverifiable_phone
- repeated_bad_feedback
- marketplace_trust_unknown

## Example output
- Vendor Safety: Medium Risk
- Why: marketplace signal exists, but identity consistency is partial and public footprint is thin
- Suggested next action: verify website and phone before relying on inventory claims
